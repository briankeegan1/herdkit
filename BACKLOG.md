(2) lane spawn — worktree + SHARE_LINKS symlinks, multi-kilobyte argv prompts; (3) build — isolated builders re-deriving context from files; (4) WATCHER TICK LOOP (`agent-watch.sh`) — how many `gh`/`herdr`/`git` subprocesses it forks per tick (latency + rate-limit + cost), what it recomputes every tick vs could cache, and whether the tick cadence itself is right; (b) SPEED-UPS (caching, parallelization, better sequencing, cheaper models/tools); (c) UNNECESSARY gates — anything duplicating GitHub's own branch-protection/required-checks or adding latency without safety value;
